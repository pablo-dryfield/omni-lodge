Privacy Policy

OmniLodge Android and related mobile operational workflows

Effective date: June 8, 2026

OmniLodge provides a mobile operations application for authorized staff and internal users. This Privacy Policy explains what information the OmniLodge Android app may process, how that information is used, and what choices are available to users.

1. Information We Process

The current Android app may process the following categories of information:

  • Account login information such as username or email address and password entered at sign in
  • Session and access information such as a backend-issued authentication token, internal user ID, and role information
  • Profile information such as username, first name, last name, and profile photo if present in the OmniLodge system
  • Notification information assigned to the signed-in user
  • Operational workspace data exposed through the OmniLodge backend, including booking, manifest, and task-planner records
  • Customer-facing booking data made available in operational flows, including names, phone numbers, booking platform, product, status, counts, and extras where returned by the backend

2. How We Use Information

  • Authenticate users and maintain signed-in sessions
  • Show role-appropriate operational data and navigation
  • Display notifications, profile details, booking activity, and task-planner records
  • Support internal hospitality, staffing, and daily operations workflows
  • Protect the service and investigate technical issues or misuse

3. Data Stored on Device

The Android app stores a session token in app-private device storage so the user can remain signed in between launches. The app does not include advertising SDKs or third-party analytics SDKs in the current codebase.

4. Sharing and Access

The app communicates with the OmniLodge backend API to authenticate users and retrieve or update operational data. Information may be accessible to authorized administrators, managers, and staff users according to permissions configured in the OmniLodge platform.

5. Permissions and Device Access

The current Android app requires network access to communicate with the OmniLodge backend. Based on the current implementation, it does not request runtime access to location, contacts, camera, microphone, SMS, call logs, or calendar data.

6. Data Retention

Most data shown in the Android app is retrieved from the OmniLodge backend. Retention of operational records is governed by backend systems and internal business processes. Locally stored session data may remain on the device until the user signs out, the app clears the session, or the app data is removed.

7. Security

We use technical and organizational measures intended to protect information processed through the app. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

8. User Choices

  • Users may sign out of the app, which clears the locally stored session token
  • Users may contact their OmniLodge administrator regarding access, correction, or removal requests related to platform data

9. Children

The OmniLodge Android app is intended for authorized staff and internal operational use and is not directed to children.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The latest version should be published at this page and referenced from relevant product materials and app store listings.
